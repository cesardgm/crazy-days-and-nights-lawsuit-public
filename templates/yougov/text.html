By The Numbers:

In order to analyze Enty Lawyer's blogging activities, we utilize a data-driven approach.

YouGov Ratings is a project by YouGov that provides nationally representative popularity scores for a multitude of categories, including individuals and corporations. The methodology is precise and accurate, based on over 20 million responses, and the data is updated weekly.

Popularity scores are calculated as percentages and the data is weighted to be nationally representative using key demographic variables like gender, race, age, and education.

Metrics

Two key metrics are utilized by YouGov: 'FAME' and 'POPULARITY'.

'FAME' corresponds to the percentage of the population familiar with a specific individual, analogous to name recognition. 'POPULARITY', on the other hand, refers to the percentage of the population with a positive opinion of an individual, similar to favorability. These metrics provide insight into the public's awareness and perception of various individuals.

However, for our purpose, we will deviate from these metrics. Instead, we'll focus on the frequency of mentions each celebrity receives on the blog "Crazy Days and Nights". We will list the celebrities in descending order based on the frequency of their mentions, excluding those with zero mentions.

Next, we calculate the cumulative number of posts made by celebrities, ranked from the most mentioned to the least mentioned. We then break down this cumulative frequency by gender. Finally, to facilitate better understanding, we convert these frequencies into densities (a number out of 1.0, where 1.0 corresponds to the total number of mentions of of all celebrities on the blog).
